<h1>CTCOVID.com Frequently Asked Questions</h1>
<p>Last Updated: February 13, 2021</p>

<hr/>

<h4>Is this website owned, operated or affiliated with any government, department, agency or health service?</h4>
<p>No, this website is operated by a private individual.</p>

<h4>Does this site provide medical advice or public health recommendations?</h4>
<p>No. Please visit the <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-nCoV/index.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Centers for Disease Control and Prevention</a> website or the <a href="https://portal.ct.gov/coronavirus" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Connecticut COVID-19 Portal</a> websites for more information regarding COVID-19.</p>

<h4>Why is hospitalization data not available for my town/city or county?</h4>
<p>Unfortunately, the State of Connecticuts' Department of Public Health only provides this information on a state-wide level. In the event that the state offers this data on a more granular level, we will add it as soon as possible.</p>

<h4>When is this site updated?</h4>
<p>The site is updated daily at approximately 5:30pm EDT. We depend on the data being available from the State of Connecticut and CDC, as a result, there may be some delay. We will usually announce on <a href="https://www.twitter.com/ctcovid" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Twitter</a> when the data has been updated.</p>

<h4>Do you guarantee the accuracy of the data on this website?</h4>
<p>No &mdash; all information on CTCOVID.com is presented without any guarantee of accuracy, correctness or completeness.</p>

<h4>Can I find out who has COVID-19 using this website?</h4>
<p>No. This website does not provide, contain or possess information about any protected health information, including names or addresses of individuals who have contracted COVID-19.</p>

<h4>How can I report a bug or request a feature?</h4>
<p>You can report a bug or request a feature on our <a href="https://www.github.com/ctcovid/ctcovid.github.io" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">GitHub</a> issues page or you can reach out to us on <a href="https://www.twitter.com/ctcovid" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Twitter</a>.</p>

<h4>Is the software behind CTCOVID.com open source?</h4>
<p>No, the software behind CTCOVID.com, except the components described on our <a href="#attribution">Attribution</a> page, is copyrighted and proprietary. However, most of source code is written in HTML and JavaScript, and as a result, is "source available"; you are free to review it for self-educational purposes on <a href="https://www.github.com/ctcovid/ctcovid.github.io" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">GitHub</a>.</p>

<h4>Do you accept tips?</h4>
<p>No. Please donate to the <a href="https://www.ctfoodbank.org/" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Connecticut Food Bank</a> or your local food pantry or soup kitchen instead.</p>

<h4>I am a member of the media, how can I contact CTCOVID.com?</h4>
<p>If you are a member of the media, you can reach out to us on <a href="https://www.twitter.com/ctcovid" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Twitter</a> and we'll get in touch with you via DM.</p>

<h4>I have another question or comment about this website, how can I contact CTCOVID.com?</h4>
<p>Please reach out to us via <a href="https://www.twitter.com/ctcovid" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow">Twitter</a> if you have any questions about this website.</p>
